## Key Trends in Aerospace Manufacturing

### 1. AI‑Driven Predictive Maintenance
Aerospace companies are shifting from scheduled maintenance to condition‑based and predictive maintenance using machine learning models.  
**Impact:** Reduces unplanned downtime and improves equipment reliability.

### 2. Advanced Composite Materials
There is rapid growth in the use of thermoplastic composites (PEEK, PEI, CFRTP) due to their strength‑to‑weight ratio and recyclability.  
**Impact:** Requires tighter control of molding parameters and moisture management.

### 3. Digital Thread and Traceability
Manufacturers are adopting digital traceability systems that track every part from raw material to final assembly.  
**Impact:** In‑mold sensors and AI inspection systems support this requirement.

### 4. Automation and Cobots
Collaborative robots are increasingly used for part handling, trimming, inspection, and assembly.  
**Impact:** Improves consistency and reduces ergonomic risks.

### 5. AI‑Enhanced Quality Inspection
Computer vision systems trained on golden batch datasets are replacing manual inspection.  
**Impact:** Higher detection accuracy and reduced human error.

### 6. Additive Manufacturing for Tooling
3D‑printed conformal cooling channels and mold inserts are becoming standard for complex aerospace geometries.  
**Impact:** Improves thermal uniformity and reduces cycle time.

### 7. Sustainability and Scrap Reduction
Regulatory pressure is pushing aerospace companies to reduce waste and improve energy efficiency.  
**Impact:** Scientific molding and resin verification are becoming mandatory.
